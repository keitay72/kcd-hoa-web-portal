# KC Disposal Fleet Operations & DOT Compliance Program

## Purpose

The purpose of this program is to establish standardized fleet management, inspection, maintenance reporting, and accountability procedures throughout KC Disposal operations.

### Goals

- Improve DOT compliance
- Reduce vehicle downtime
- Lower maintenance costs
- Improve driver accountability
- Increase route reliability
- Extend equipment lifespan
- Improve workplace safety

---

# Organizational Accountability

## Driver Responsibilities

Drivers are responsible for:

- Completing daily pre-trip inspections
- Completing daily post-trip inspections
- Reporting equipment defects
- Maintaining vehicle cleanliness
- Fueling vehicles
- Refilling DEF
- Operating equipment safely

Drivers are the first line of defense against equipment failures.

---

## Lead Driver Responsibilities

Lead Drivers are responsible for:

- Oversight of assigned drivers
- Spot-check inspections
- Coaching and mentoring
- Verifying inspection compliance
- Monitoring safety compliance

Lead Drivers serve as the first level of operational accountability.

---

## Supervisor Responsibilities

Supervisors are responsible for:

- Daily operational oversight
- Driver accountability
- Audit reviews
- Corrective actions
- Performance monitoring

---

## Operations Manager Responsibilities

Operations Managers are responsible for:

- Fleet readiness
- Route execution
- Resource allocation
- KPI reporting
- Operational compliance

---

## Fleet Manager Responsibilities

Fleet Managers are responsible for:

- Preventive maintenance scheduling
- Repair coordination
- Maintenance compliance
- Fleet lifecycle management

---

## Safety Manager Responsibilities

Safety Managers are responsible for:

- DOT compliance
- Safety audits
- Driver training
- Incident investigations
- Regulatory reporting

---

# Driver End-of-Shift Accountability

Before leaving the facility, drivers shall answer the following questions:

1. Did you dump all loads?
2. Did you clean behind the blade?
3. Did you fuel the truck?
4. Did you refill DEF?
5. Did you complete your post-trip inspection?
6. Did you report all known defects?

Failure to report known defects may result in disciplinary action.

---

# Daily Vehicle Inspection Requirements

## Pre-Trip Inspection

The pre-trip inspection shall be completed before vehicle operation.

### Vehicle Systems

Inspect:

- Tires
- Wheels
- Lug nuts
- Suspension
- Steering components
- Brakes
- Lights
- Reflectors
- Mirrors
- Windshield
- Wipers
- Horn
- Backup alarm
- Camera systems

### Fluid Levels

Inspect:

- Engine oil
- Coolant
- Hydraulic fluid
- Transmission fluid
- Fuel
- DEF

### Safety Equipment

Verify:

- Fire extinguisher
- Safety triangles
- First aid supplies
- Required PPE

---

## Post-Trip Inspection

Drivers shall:

- Remove debris from vehicle
- Clean behind blade
- Empty hopper as required
- Refuel vehicle
- Refill DEF
- Complete DVIR
- Report all defects

---

# Automated Side Loader Arm Inspection

## Functional Test

Prior to route departure:

### Arm Function

- Extend arms three times
- Retract arms three times

Verify smooth operation and no abnormal noise.

### Carriage Function

- Raise carriage three times
- Lower carriage three times

Verify no binding or abnormal movement.

### Claw Function

- Open claw three times
- Close claw three times

Verify full travel and proper gripping function.

---

## Physical Inspection

### Hydraulic Hoses and Fittings

Inspect for:

- Leaks
- Cracks
- Dry rot
- Abrasion
- Damaged braid
- Loose fittings

### Grabber Fingers

Verify:

- Tightness
- No missing hardware
- No cracks
- No excessive wear

### Carriage Rollers

Verify:

- Proper contact
- No excessive play
- No abnormal wear

### Rear Motor Assembly

Verify:

- Tight mounting
- No leaks
- No broken bolts
- No missing bolts

### Front Motor Assembly

Perform the same inspection as the rear motor.

### Side Rollers ("Candy Cane")

Verify:

- Secure mounting
- No excessive movement
- No abnormal wear
- No bearing failure

# Defect Reporting Standards

Accurate reporting reduces repair time and downtime.

## Required Information

Every defect report must include:

| Field | Requirement |
|---------|---------|
| Unit Number | Required |
| Date | Required |
| Location of Issue | Required |
| Description | Required |
| Severity | Required |
| Driver Name | Required |

## Examples

### Poor Report

> Truck leaking.

### Good Report

> Hydraulic leak observed at upper arm cylinder connection behind carriage.

### Good Report

> Passenger-side rear outside tire below minimum tread depth.

### Good Report

> Air leak detected at brake chamber fitting on driver's side rear axle.

# Corrective Action Policy

## First Occurrence

Coaching and documented discussion.

## Second Occurrence

Written warning.

## Third Occurrence

Final warning and Performance Improvement Plan (PIP).

## Continued Non-Compliance

Further disciplinary action up to and including termination.
